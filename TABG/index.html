That Awkward Blind Girl

Navigating life as a Stargardtien.

Hello, World

My name is Miranda Loughry. I’m a student, a dancer, a writer, and an amateur programmer. Also, I’m legally blind.

I can remember struggling to see clearly when I was in first or second grade. I knew I couldn’t function in the same way as my classmates, but I was afraid to ask anyone for help. A few years later, just after my tenth birthday, my parents took me to an optometrist when I couldn’t pass a simple vision test my pediatrician administered. After running copious diagnostic tests, the optometrist referred me to a specialist, and the specialist told me that I have Stargardt disease.

At first, I was confused. I didn’t know what would happen next, and I was afraid of the future. How quickly would my vision degenerate? Would I be able to operate without the full use of my eyes? How could I navigate a world built for sighted people? I didn’t have all the answers then, and I’m afraid I never will. I’m figuring them out as I go along, solving each problem as it confronts me. And you know what? Everyone else is, too. Stargardt has taught me many things, but I think the most important two are these:

  1. Everyone has something that challenges them, so honesty and kindness are paramount.
  2. Sunglasses are cool.

So, What's Stargardt?
